Challenge Author Code of Conduct

Thank you for contributing challenges to CodeLighthouse! As a challenge author, you play a crucial role in creating a positive and educational experience for the community. By submitting challenges, you agree to adhere to this Code of Conduct. This Code applies to all challenge submissions and interactions within the CodeLighthouse community.

1. Clarity and Consistency

Write challenges with clarity and maintain a consistent format. Ensure that the challenge description, input/output specifications, and examples are easy to understand. Use a consistent and clear style throughout your challenge.

2. Inclusivity

Create challenges that are inclusive and accessible to a diverse audience. Avoid using language or examples that may be exclusive or offensive to any individual or group. Consider different perspectives and experiences when crafting your challenges.

3. Educational Value

Prioritize the educational value of challenges. Aim to provide opportunities for learning and growth. Include clear explanations, relevant context, and encourage participants to explore different approaches to problem-solving.

4. Fairness

Design challenges that are fair to all participants. Avoid ambiguous or misleading statements in the challenge description. Ensure that all participants have an equal chance to understand and solve the problem.

5. Test Cases

Include a reasonable number of test cases to validate solutions. Test cases should cover a variety of scenarios, including edge cases. Clearly specify the expected output for each test case. Randomized tests should be well-defined to ensure consistency in evaluation.

6. Avoiding Traps

Avoid creating challenges with intentional traps or misdirection. Challenges should test participants' understanding and problem-solving skills rather than trying to confuse or mislead them.

7. Solution Space

Consider a diverse solution space. Encourage participants to explore different algorithms and approaches to solving the problem. Avoid restrictions that limit creativity without good reason.

8. Timeliness

Be responsive to questions and feedback from participants. If participants seek clarification on your challenge, respond promptly and provide additional information if necessary.

9. Code of Conduct Compliance

Ensure that your challenges comply with the overall Code of Conduct for the CodeLighthouse community. Report any issues or violations promptly to the CodeLighthouse administrators.

10. Continuous Improvement

Seek feedback on your challenges and be open to making improvements. CodeLighthouse is a collaborative platform, and continuous improvement is key to providing a positive experience for all participants.
